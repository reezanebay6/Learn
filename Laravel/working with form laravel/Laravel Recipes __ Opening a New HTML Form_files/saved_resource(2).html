            J. Alexander Curtis mjfragale • 2 years ago

            you are mixing up GET and POST methods. You can not do both, even outside of laravel. Its just against HTML. So if you want to put parameters onto your URL then you will want to change the method to "GET" because then all your form data will be added as parameters like you wish. But if you want post, then just add those parameters as fields in your form (maybe try hidden fields if you dont want users to select them) and then when you submit the form those parameters will be passed over in the POST field.

                          Chuck Heintzelman Mod Carolyn Foo • 2 years ago

                          No it has to go through the routes. The problem is that it equates to a URL, a HTTP request. Then that HTTP request will then transfer control to the appropriate controller and action. There's no mechanism in Laravel to take a URL and automatically translate it to a controller/action. It has to go through the routing, the the reverse of this (converting an action to a URL) also needs to look at the routing to do it correctly.
